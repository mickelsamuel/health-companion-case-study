1. Introduction

Managing chronic health conditions often involves juggling multiple medications, doctor appointments, nutrition tracking, and mental wellness checks. The Health Companion Super App aims to simplify this process by consolidating these tasks into one seamless platform, fostering better health outcomes and improved user adherence.

Scope: This case study documents the research, design, and testing process behind building an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) for the Health Companion Super App. The app features medication reminders, appointment scheduling, and additional wellness tools (e.g., mental health support, accountability features).

2. User Research

We conducted an online survey with 40 participants and 5 in-depth interviews to understand user needs:

Medication Tracking Methods

70% use manual methods (like pillboxes), while 30% rely on mobile apps for medication tracking.

Frequency of Forgotten Doses

60% frequently forget at least one dose per week. Only 15% rarely forget.

Most Requested Features

  • Appointment scheduling & easy rescheduling (65%)
  • Simplified medication reminders (58%)
  • Family sharing & accountability (35%)
  • Mental wellness tips (30%)

Top requested features from user survey

Key Insights: Users want a single, user-friendly app to handle both medications and appointments. Many also expressed interest in mental wellness resources (e.g., relaxation exercises, community support).

7. Usability Testing

We conducted usability testing with 6 participants who matched our personas‚Äô age range and health conditions. Each participant was asked to:

  • Create an account or log in (if they had existing credentials)
  • Set a new medication reminder for the next day
  • Schedule a doctor‚Äôs appointment for an available time slot
  • Access a mental wellness ‚Äúrelaxation exercise‚Äù

After analyzing results, we simplified the appointment scheduler (combining ‚ÄúNew‚Äù and ‚ÄúReschedule‚Äù into a single ‚ÄúManage‚Äù interface). We also added real-time form validation for the sign-up flow.

8. Reflection & Future Steps

Challenges: Reaching older adults for interviews was difficult, so we partnered with local clinics to gather more representative data. Additionally, balancing multiple features (medications, appointments, mental wellness) without overwhelming the user required careful iterative testing.

Successes: Our iterative approach and usability testing helped us prioritize the most-used features. The final prototype tested well, with a 90% overall task completion rate and high satisfaction scores.

Next Steps:

  • Implement family-sharing and accountability features for group management
  • Improve data visualization for daily/weekly medication adherence
  • Enhance mental wellness section with more resources and professional support channels
  • Explore EHR (Electronic Health Records) integration for seamless patient-doctor communication

9. Conclusion

The Health Companion Super App demonstrates how a user-centered design process can simplify complex healthcare needs. By focusing on straightforward medication tracking, flexible appointment scheduling, and a supportive mental wellness toolkit, we aim to empower users to take control of their health every day.
